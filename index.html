How to Play & Prolog logic

The Mystery

Dr. Black has been murdered! The crime took place at 10:00 PM in one of the castle rooms. Your job is to find the Culprit, the Crime Scene Room, and the Murder Weapon.

Gathering Clues

  • Search Rooms: Click on different rooms on the map, then click "Search Room". You will find physical trace evidence (shoe size prints, hair color fibers, fingerprint types). Physical clues are always 100% true!
  • Interview Suspects: Suspects move between rooms. Click on their avatar button in the room panel to interview them. They will tell you their alibi (where they claim to have been at 10 PM) or mention seeing someone else.

The Prolog Reasoning Engine

Every clue or statement you discover is asserted as a dynamic Prolog fact (e.g. clue_found(footprint, library, 9) or alibi_claimed(mustard, kitchen, 10)).

When you click Run Prolog Reasoner, the system executes real Prolog queries over these facts:

  • innocent(X, Reason): A suspect is innocent if physical evidence at the crime scene contradicts their details, OR if they have a verified alibi, OR if physical evidence places them in a different room at 10 PM.
  • contradiction(Msg): Looks for statements that conflict with physical trace evidence (meaning that suspect is lying!) or statements that contradict each other.
  • guilty(X, Reason): A suspect is guilty if they are caught lying, or if they are the only suspect who cannot be proven innocent!

Deduction Rules to Keep in Mind

Rule of Thumb: Physical clues are absolute truths. Innocent characters always tell the truth about where they were. The killer always lies about their own alibi! If a suspect's alibi statement conflicts with physical evidence, they are caught lying and are the killer!
